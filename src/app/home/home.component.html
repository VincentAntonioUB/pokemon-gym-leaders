Kanto Region

The Kanto region is the inaugural, temperate, and heavily urbanized setting from the Generation I Pokémon games, heavily inspired by Japan's real-world Kantō region. Known for its central, modern cities (like Saffron and Celadon) connected by linear routes, it features diverse landmarks such as Viridian Forest, Mt. Moon, and Indigo Plateau.

Johto Region

The Johto region, setting for Pokémon Gold, Silver, Crystal, and their remakes, is located directly west of Kanto, sharing a, and heavily inspired by Japan's traditional Kansai region. Known for its rural, historical atmosphere, Johto features old-fashioned cities like Ecruteak and Violet City, contrasted with modern hubs like Goldenrod City.
